Tokyo‑Ga - The Contrast of Japan in the 80s

By Wim Wenders · February 18, 2026 · 6 min read

Wenders wanders a city racing forward, searching for Ozu's stillness. In 1980s Tokyo, the German director found a metropolis caught between tradition and hyper-modernity.

The Search for Ozu's Tokyo

When Wim Wenders traveled to Tokyo in the early 1980s, he wasn't just making a documentary about the city—he was searching for the ghost of Yasujirō Ozu, the master director whose films captured a Japan that was already fading. What he found was a city in frantic transformation...

"I had the feeling that Tokyo was racing forward so fast that it was leaving its own shadow behind."

A City of Contrasts

The Tokyo Wenders captured on film was one of pachinko parlors next to ancient temples, businessmen in suits alongside teenagers in avant-garde fashion. It was this tension—between tradition and progress, stillness and motion—that made Tokyo‑Ga such a prescient work...
